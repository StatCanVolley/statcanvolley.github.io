SCMVL Rules
These are the official rules of the Statistics Canada Mixed Volleyball League.
1. General
1.1- The maximum number of players on the court is 6.
 a) The maximum number of men on the court is 3.
 b) No maximum for women - as long as the number of players does not exceed 6.
1.2- The minimum number of players on the court is 4.
 a) No minimum for men - as long as the number of players is not less than 4.
 b) The minimum number of women on the court is 2.
1.3- If a team does not have the minimum of 4 players, 15 minutes after the official start time, three options are available to the opposing captain:
 a) win 5-0 by default and not play the game
 b) play the game and accept the score as is
 c) win 5-0 by default and play the game for fun if the opponent team want.
 d) any other arrangement agreed to by both captains.
 Note: The official start time is the time indicated on the schedule.
1.4- Eligibility
 a) All men who work at or have worked at Statistics Canada are eligible to play
 b) All men who have played in the league prior to 1998.
 c) No restrictions for women
1.5- Spares
 a) During the regular season, any person can spare on any team, as long as they are eligible according to SCMVL rule number 1.4 . Please refrain from stacking your team.
 b) During the playoffs, any person can spare on any team, as long as they are eligible according to SCMVL rule number 1.4 and do not play on regular basis on a team from a higher division. Please refrain from stacking your team.
 c) Any exceptions (due to extenuating circumstances) to the spares rules must be agreed to by the captains prior to showing up at the game.
1.6- Team Rosters
 a) Any eligible person according to SCMVL rule number 1.4 , but your team can not consist of more than one man and one woman (or two women) regular players on a team from a higher division.
2. Referees
2.1- There are no outside referees
2.2- Each player is responsible for calling his/her faults (Touching the net, lifting, …)
 Note: Some flexibility/exceptions should be made for certain inexperienced/weaker players in the B and C division.
2.3- Only captains can discuss calls
2.4- If no agreement can be reached, the point will be replayed.
 Note: Do not forget that it is a fun league.
3. Playing Rules
3.1- A game consist of 5 sets of 25 points. Every exchange result in a point (no need to have the serve).
3.2- A game have to complete with a difference of 2 points up to a maximum of 31 points.
3.3- The home team (on the right-hand side of the schedule) serves first.
3.4- A maximum of 5 serves in a row by the same player. If this happens, the serving team will rotate and the next player will continue with the serve.
3.5- The ball is fair if it touches the net on the serve.
3.6- The official volleyball rules apply for all other cases.
4. Equipment
4.1- The home team (on the right-hand side of the schedule) is responsible for bringing the ball.
4.2- The location of the balls, is indicated on the Web-page under the contacts section.
4.3- The winning team must report the score to standings Responsible via e-mail as soon as possible.
5. Gymnasium Information
5.1- Don't walk with your outdoor's shoes in the gymnasium.
5.2- The rental period is two hours and includes time to set up the net and to remove it.
5.3- Teams should arrive for the start of the rental. Later you start the game, the less time you may have to complete it. The period of 15 minutes is not to start the game but to apply the rule 1.3.
 a) If the game starts later than 15 minutes of delay and the game is not over, the team that caused the delay will lose all the sets that have not been completed / played.
5.4- The games must be completed 5 minutes before the end of the rental in order to remove the net.
 a) The team leading at that time wins the set. Rule 5.3 a) will override this rule.
 b) If it's tied, make a final rally to determine the winner.
6. League Information
6.1- League Re-alignment
 a) Switching teams from one division to another will be made in the best interest of the team and the league.
6.2- An accounting of the revenues and expenses will be made available. League finances are controlled by the treasurer.
7. Directives - Not a rule (When spiking)
Division A You should be good enough to find the holes and not hit anyone directly.
Division B You should not be hitting the ball with more than three quarters of your strength. Although more experienced, some of these players can easily be hurt and should not be used as target practice.
Division C You should not be hitting the ball with more than half of your strength. Many of the players in C Division are inexperienced and should not be used as target practice.
